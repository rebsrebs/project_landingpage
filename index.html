Goldfish Myths

Myth: Goldfish don't live very long.

Fact: Goldfish have an average lifespan of 10-15 years and can live decades longer, but they often die young in captivity because of inadequate environment and care.1

Myth: Goldfish have a 3 second memory.

Fact: Goldfish have been proven to have memories of at least five months2, but anecdotally their memory has been shown to last over years.3

Myth: Goldfish can be kept in bowls.

Fact: The bowl shape actually is harmful to fish.4 Most goldfish need a minimum of 20 gallons of water, but some need more than 50, and that's for one fish. Multiple fish require larger tanks or ponds.5

Myth: Goldfish should live alone.

Fact: Goldfish are social creatures who thrive best with other fish as long as they are of the appropriate species.5
